## HOOKS AND OPENING SECONDS

### The 1.3-Second Rule (2025-2026)

You have **1.3 seconds** to capture attention. The first 3 seconds are critical for algorithmic success.

**Hook Success Statistics:**
- 65% who watch first 3 seconds will watch 10+ seconds
- 45% who watch first 3 seconds will watch 30+ seconds
- 33% of TikTok viewers scroll past in first 3 seconds
- 60% engagement drop on Instagram without opening hook
- Average human attention span: 8.25 seconds (25% decrease since 2003)

### Hook Formula (Second-by-Second)

| Second | Purpose | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| 0-1 | Visual/Audio Grab | Movement, pattern interrupt, unexpected visual |
| 1-2 | Value Proposition | What they'll get or learn |
| 2-3 | Curiosity Gap | Open loop that demands resolution |

### Psychological Triggers for Hooks

1. **Curiosity Gap**: Create an information gap that viewers must fill
2. **Pattern Interruption**: Break expected patterns to reset attention (triggers orienting response)
3. **Open Loops (Zeigarnik Effect)**: Start stories that demand completion
4. **Loss Aversion/FOMO**: Imply they'll miss out if they leave
5. **Self-Identification**: "If you're someone who..." immediately qualifies viewers
6. **Variable Reward**: Promise unexpected value or outcome

### Hook Templates

**Curiosity Hooks:**
- "Here's why [common belief] is completely wrong..."
- "I discovered something that changed everything about..."
- "Most people don't know this about..."

**Pattern Interrupt Hooks:**
- Start mid-action or mid-sentence
- Begin with an unexpected visual or sound
- Use a controversial statement

**Open Loop Hooks:**
- "By the end of this video, you'll understand why..."
- "What happened next shocked everyone..."
- "The third tip is the one that actually works..."

**FOMO Hooks:**
- "If you're not doing this, you're losing..."
- "This trend is about to explode..."
- "Before this gets taken down..."
